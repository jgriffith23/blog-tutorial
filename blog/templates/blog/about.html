About

Hi, I’m Jennifer. I have a degree in electrical engineering, I spent several years editing technical books, and for a while, I was a freelance technical editor and writer.

I still enjoy writing and editing, but since graduating from Hackbright Academy, I’ve changed careers. Now, I’m a software engineer and lab instructor at Hackbright. I still like building hardware stuff, too; I’m just much better at programming these days, and I can also build webapps.

I started my original GitHub blog to document my experiences at Hackbright. Post-graduation, I built this Django site, am moving the material over (still a work in progress), and will continue using the blog to:

  • Reflect on experiences I have in the tech space
  • Keep my writing skills (both technical and non-technical) fresh
  • Practice explaining concepts about software and hardware

When I’m not coding or editing, you can find me poking at stuff with a multimeter (or sonic screwdriver), on a crafting adventure involving LEDs and thread, out for a run, or enjoying some tea.

Allons-y!
